# SchoolPurse — Brand Voice & Messaging Guidelines
_LLM-ready. Source: launch posts, demand plan, product testimonials, dashboard handoff. Generated 2026-06-03._

## 1. One-line positioning
SchoolPurse is the internal finance dashboard a Zimbabwean school office actually uses — to track every fee, issue every receipt, and see every report — built for bursars, heads and boards, not for "users."

**Tagline (locked):** Track every fee. Issue every receipt. See every report.

## 2. Audience (who we speak to)
| Role | Cares about | Speak to them with |
|------|-------------|--------------------|
| Bursar / accounts clerk | Less manual reconciling, instant receipts, fewer mistakes | Practical relief, respect for their craft |
| Head teacher / School Head | Money under control, arrears handled quietly, board-ready reports | Calm authority, trust, discretion |
| Board treasurer | Accurate P&L, isolation, audit trail | Precision, governance, restraint |

We address the **person running the office**, never a generic "user." Default reader: a busy bursar with intermittent internet and a roster spreadsheet.

## 3. Voice pillars
1. **Clarity over cleverness** — plain words, concrete nouns. If a sentence needs decoding, rewrite it.
2. **Restraint** — say less. One claim, well made, beats five. No hype, no exclamation pile-ups.
3. **Demonstrated trust** — we show, not assert: isolation, roles, audit-logging, amounts in words on receipts.
4. **Human & respectful** — we talk to professionals about their real Friday-night work, warmly and without condescension.
5. **Local by design** — Zimbabwe is the default context, not a footnote: cash + mobile money, USD fees, offline-first.

## 4. We Are / We Are Not
| We ARE | We are NOT |
|--------|------------|
| Calm, plain, exact | Hype-y, salesy, buzzword-y |
| Practical ("record a payment, issue the receipt") | Abstract ("leverage synergies", "revolutionise") |
| Respectful of school staff and their craft | Condescending or "we'll save you from yourselves" |
| Trust shown through specifics (isolation, audit) | Trust claimed through adjectives ("secure!", "powerful!") |
| Local and specific (Form 1–6, EcoCash, term fees) | Generic global SaaS English |
| Quietly confident | Loud, urgent, FOMO-driven |
| Warm and human | Cold, robotic, or jokey/meme-y about money |

## 6. Lexicon
**Use:** fee, receipt, arrears, balance, term, levy, bursar, head teacher, office, record, issue, reconcile, isolated, audit-logged, mobile money, EcoCash, USD, Form 1–6, P&L, surplus.
**Prefer:** "school office" / "your bursar" over "users"; "record a payment" over "process a transaction"; "see" / "watch" over "gain visibility into"; "built for" over "designed to empower."
**Avoid:** revolutionise, seamless, cutting-edge, game-changer, supercharge, leverage, robust, powerful (as filler), unlock, empower, synergy, next-gen, AI-powered (unless literally true and relevant), "users."
**Currency:** USD only (v1). Always render money with `formatMoney` style ("$51.25", "amount in words" on receipts). Don't imply multi-currency yet.

## 7. Mechanics
- **Spelling:** Commonwealth/Zimbabwean English — "colour", "organise", "recognise", "cheque" no→ "cheque". (Not US spelling.)
- **Numbers:** Forms as "Form 1–6"; use en-dash for ranges (31–60 days). Money with "$" prefix.
- **Punctuation:** em-dash for the calm aside (" — and quietly followed up."). Sparing exclamation marks (max one per piece, usually zero).
- **Capitalisation:** Sentence case for headlines and UI. "SchoolPurse" always one word, camel-cap; "Purse" may render in green when styled.
- **Emoji:** sparing. 🇿🇼 for local pride, 🧾 receipts, 🔒 trust. Never more than 1–2 per caption. No money-bag/clown/hype emoji.
- **Handle:** @SchoolPursezw. **Link:** "link in bio" (route to WhatsApp/sign-in).

## 8. Messaging hierarchy (what to lead with)
1. **Relief from manual work** — "Friday-night receipt reconciling is ending."
2. **The three verbs** — track / issue / see.
3. **Arrears, handled quietly** — colour-coded, act before small becomes hard.
4. **Trust** — isolated per school, roles, audit-logged.
5. **Local fit** — cash + mobile money, USD fees, offline sync.
Lead with 1–2 for cold audiences; 3–4 for warm/evaluating; 5 reinforces throughout.

## 9. Do / Don't examples
**Headline**
- ✅ "SchoolPurse is live."
- ✅ "Built for Zimbabwean schools."
- ❌ "Revolutionise your school's financial management today!"

**Benefit line**
- ✅ "Record a payment, issue the receipt, send it to the parent — before they've left the desk."
- ❌ "Streamline your end-to-end payment lifecycle with our powerful platform."

**Trust**
- ✅ "Every school's data is fully isolated. Every action is audit-logged."
- ❌ "Bank-grade, military-strength, ultra-secure infrastructure."

**Testimonial framing (real voice to emulate)**
- ✅ "The arrears view alone paid for the year. We saw three families in critical status the first morning and quietly followed up."
- ❌ Polished marketing quotes with no specifics.
