# How to manage school fees in Zimbabwe

Managing school fees in Zimbabwe is hard. Most schools still collect cash at the office and write each payment into a paper receipt book. At the end of term nobody really knows how much came in or who still owes. This guide explains a better way to manage school fees, step by step.

## Why cash and paper cause problems

When fees are tracked on paper, mistakes happen. Receipts get lost. Two people write in the same book. Parents say they paid but there is no record. The bursar spends days reconciling the cash book instead of running the school. Arrears pile up because nobody can see them clearly.

## Step 1: Decide your fee items

First, list everything you charge for the term. Tuition, levy, sports, ICT, exams, transport. Decide which classes each fee applies to and whether it is charged per term, per month, or once.

## Step 2: Invoice every student

Once your fee items are set, create an invoice for each active student for the current term. The invoice should list the fees that apply to that student's class.

## Step 3: Record every payment

When a parent pays, record the amount, the date, and the method. In Zimbabwe parents pay by cash, bank transfer, or mobile money like EcoCash. Give the parent a receipt with a unique number.

## Step 4: Track arrears

After payments are recorded, you can see which students still owe money. Sort by the largest balance so you can follow up with those parents first.

## Step 5: Review your collection rate

At any point you should be able to see how much of the term's fees have been collected versus what is still outstanding.
